Smoky Vegan Corn Chowder with Potatoes

Prep: 15 min | Cook: 30 min | Total: 45 min

Full Ingredients List:

  • 4 ears fresh corn (or 3 cups frozen corn kernels)
  • 4 medium Yukon gold potatoes, diced into ½-inch cubes
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegan butter
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (plus extra for garnish)
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (low sodium if preferred)
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves (or 1 tsp dried)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh lime juice (1-2 tablespoons)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Detailed Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: If using fresh corn, carefully cut kernels from cobs using a sharp knife. Set aside. Dice potatoes, onion, bell pepper, and celery. Mince garlic.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and celery, sautéing for 5-7 minutes until onions become translucent and slightly golden at edges.
  3. Build Flavor Base: Add minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, and cayenne (if using). Stir constantly for 1 minute until garlic is fragrant and spices are toasted but not burned.
  4. Add Potatoes and Corn: Stir in diced potatoes and corn kernels, coating them thoroughly with the spice mixture. Cook for 3-4 minutes to lightly toast the corn.
  5. Add Liquid and Simmer: Pour in vegetable broth and add diced red bell pepper and thyme leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes until potatoes are fork-tender.
  6. Blend for Creaminess: Remove about 2 cups of the soup (focusing on solids) and carefully blend until smooth using an immersion blender or countertop blender. Return blended portion to pot. This creates a creamy base while maintaining chunky texture.
  7. Finish with Coconut Milk: Stir in coconut milk and simmer uncovered for 5 more minutes. The soup should thicken slightly. Add lime juice and season generously with salt and black pepper to taste.
  8. Final Adjustments: Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. If soup is too thick, add ¼ cup of water or broth. If too thin, simmer 5 more minutes uncovered.
  9. Serve: Ladle into bowls, garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley, a sprinkle of smoked paprika, and a lime wedge on the side. Serve with crusty bread for dipping.
  10. Storage Tips: This soup tastes even better the next day! Store in airtight container for up to 5 days, or freeze for 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop, adding a splash of water if too thick.

Cheesy Vegan Broccoli & Barley with White Beans

Prep: 15 min | Cook: 35 min | Total: 50 min

Full Ingredients List:

  • 1 cup pearl barley, rinsed
  • 1 large head broccoli (about 1 lb), cut into florets
  • 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked 2 hours or boiled 10 min)
  • ½ cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cups vegetable broth (divided)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric (for color)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil (divided)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Detailed Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Prepare Cashews: Soak cashews in hot water for at least 2 hours, or boil for 10 minutes until softened. Drain and set aside. (This step can be done ahead of time.)
  2. Cook Barley: In a medium saucepan, combine rinsed barley with 2 cups vegetable broth. Bring to boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes until barley is tender and liquid is absorbed. Fluff with fork and set aside.
  3. Make Cheese Sauce: In a high-speed blender, combine soaked cashews, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, turmeric, ½ teaspoon salt, and 1 cup vegetable broth. Blend on high until completely smooth and creamy (2-3 minutes). Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more broth if too thick.
  4. Prepare Broccoli: While barley cooks, steam broccoli florets for 5-6 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Immediately rinse under cold water to stop cooking and preserve color. Set aside.
  5. Sauté Aromatics: Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and sauté for 5-6 minutes until soft and translucent. Add minced garlic and cook 1 more minute until fragrant.
  6. Combine Ingredients: Add cooked barley, steamed broccoli, and cannellini beans to the skillet with onions. Gently stir to combine without mashing beans.
  7. Add Cheese Sauce: Pour the cashew cheese sauce over the barley mixture. Stir gently to coat everything evenly. Cook over medium-low heat for 3-4 minutes until everything is heated through.
  8. Season and Finish: Season with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. If mixture seems dry, add a splash of vegetable broth or water. Stir in remaining tablespoon of olive oil for richness.
  9. Serve: Divide among bowls, garnish with fresh parsley and extra nutritional yeast if desired. Serve immediately while warm.
  10. Expert Tips: For a nut-free version, substitute raw sunflower seeds for cashews. To make this gluten-free, use quinoa instead of barley. This dish reheats beautifully - store in airtight container for up to 4 days.

30-Minute Easy Lentil Bolognese

Prep: 15 min | Cook: 25 min | Total: 40 min

Full Ingredients List:

  • 1 cup brown lentils, rinsed (or 2 cups cooked)
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium carrots, finely diced
  • 2 stalks celery, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • ½ cup dry red wine (optional, substitute with broth)
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup or sugar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • 12 oz pasta of choice
  • Vegan parmesan for serving (optional)

Detailed Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Prepare Lentils (if using dry): In a medium saucepan, combine lentils with 2 cups water. Bring to boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes until tender but not mushy. Drain any excess water and set aside. (Skip this step if using cooked lentils.)
  2. Make the "Soffritto": Finely chop onion, carrots, and celery into uniform small pieces (this is key for authentic texture). Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped vegetables and sauté for 8-10 minutes until very soft and fragrant, stirring occasionally.
  3. Build Flavor Base: Add minced garlic, tomato paste, oregano, basil, thyme, and bay leaf. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until tomato paste darkens slightly and garlic is fragrant.
  4. Deglaze (Optional but Recommended): If using wine, pour it in now and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Cook until reduced by half, about 2 minutes. If not using wine, use ¼ cup vegetable broth instead.
  5. Add Tomatoes and Lentils: Stir in crushed tomatoes, cooked lentils, remaining vegetable broth, maple syrup, and red pepper flakes if using. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Simmer to Develop Flavor: Reduce heat to low, partially cover, and let simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken but still be saucy. If it gets too thick, add a splash of water or broth.
  7. Cook Pasta: While sauce simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Reserve ½ cup pasta water before draining.
  8. Final Adjustments: Remove bay leaf from sauce. Taste and season generously with salt and black pepper. For extra richness, stir in a tablespoon of olive oil or vegan butter at the end.
  9. Combine: Add drained pasta directly to the sauce along with ¼ cup of reserved pasta water. Toss well to combine, allowing pasta to absorb some sauce for 1-2 minutes over low heat.
  10. Serve: Divide among bowls, garnish with fresh basil, vegan parmesan if using, and a drizzle of good quality olive oil. Serve immediately with crusty bread on the side.
  11. Make-Ahead Tip: This sauce freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Make a double batch and freeze in portions for quick meals.

Farro Chickpea Skillet with Tomatoes, Kale, Cardamom & Lime

Prep: 20 min | Cook: 35 min | Total: 55 min

Full Ingredients List:

  • 1 cup farro, rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 bunch curly kale, stems removed, leaves chopped
  • 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil (divided)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • ½ cup vegetable broth
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh mint, chopped (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • ¼ cup toasted pine nuts or almonds for garnish
  • Vegan feta or avocado for serving (optional)

Detailed Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Cook Farro: In a medium saucepan, combine farro with 2½ cups water and a pinch of salt. Bring to boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes until farro is tender but still chewy. Drain any excess water and set aside.
  2. Toast Spices: While farro cooks, heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add cardamom, cumin, and cinnamon. Toast for 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn. Immediately transfer to a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: In the same skillet, add remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil. Add sliced red onion and cook for 6-8 minutes until soft and beginning to caramelize.
  4. Add Garlic and Tomatoes: Add minced garlic to the skillet and cook 1 minute until fragrant. Add halved cherry tomatoes and cook for 5-6 minutes until they start to soften and release their juices.
  5. Add Chickpeas: Stir in chickpeas and the toasted spice mixture. Cook for 3-4 minutes, allowing chickpeas to warm through and absorb the spices.
  6. Incorporate Farro: Add cooked farro to the skillet, stirring well to combine all ingredients. If mixture seems dry, add vegetable broth to create a light sauce.
  7. Wilt Kale: Add chopped kale to the skillet. Cover with a lid and cook for 3-4 minutes until kale wilts but remains bright green. Remove lid and stir to combine.
  8. Add Fresh Elements: Remove skillet from heat. Stir in lime zest, lime juice, chopped cilantro, and mint if using. The residual heat will soften the herbs without cooking them.
  9. Season and Garnish: Season generously with salt and black pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more lime juice if needed. Top with toasted pine nuts or almonds.
  10. Serve: Serve warm as a main dish or hearty side. For extra creaminess, top with crumbled vegan feta or sliced avocado. This dish is excellent warm, at room temperature, or even cold the next day.
  11. Variation Ideas: For a quicker version, use quinoa instead of farro (cooks in 15 minutes). Add roasted sweet potatoes for extra sweetness, or swap kale for spinach (add at the very end).

Protein-Packed Macro Bowl with Lemon Tahini Dressing

Prep: 25 min | Cook: 20 min | Total: 45 min

Full Ingredients List:

  • For the Bowl Base:
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small sweet potato, cubed
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cups shredded purple cabbage
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cucumber, sliced
  • ¼ cup pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • For the Lemon Tahini Dressing:
  • ¼ cup tahini (well-stirred)
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice (1 large lemon)
  • 2 tablespoons water (or more for desired consistency)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • Pinch of salt
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (parsley, dill, or cilantro)

Detailed Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Cook Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa with 2 cups water and a pinch of salt. Bring to boil, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let sit covered for 5 minutes, then fluff with fork.
  2. Roast Sweet Potato and Chickpeas: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss cubed sweet potato and chickpeas with 1 tablespoon olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread on baking sheet in single layer. Roast for 20 minutes, flipping halfway, until sweet potatoes are tender and chickpeas are crispy.
  3. Prepare Broccoli: While sweet potatoes roast, steam broccoli florets for 4-5 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Immediately rinse under cold water to preserve color and texture. Set aside.
  4. Make Tahini Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, water, minced garlic, maple syrup, cumin, and salt until smooth and creamy. The dressing will thicken at first but thin out as you whisk. Add more water 1 tablespoon at a time until you reach a pourable consistency (should be like heavy cream). Taste and adjust seasoning.
  5. Prepare Raw Vegetables: Thinly slice purple cabbage using a sharp knife or mandoline. Halve cherry tomatoes. Slice cucumber. Cut avocado in half, remove pit, and slice flesh while still in skin, then scoop out with spoon.
  6. Toast Pumpkin Seeds: In a small dry skillet over medium heat, toast pumpkin seeds for 2-3 minutes until they start to pop and become fragrant. Watch carefully to prevent burning. Set aside.
  7. Assemble Bowls: Divide cooked quinoa among 2-3 bowls. Arrange roasted sweet potatoes and chickpeas, steamed broccoli, purple cabbage, avocado slices, cherry tomatoes, and cucumber slices in sections around the quinoa.
  8. Add Finishing Touches: Drizzle generous amount of tahini dressing over each bowl. Sprinkle with toasted pumpkin seeds and fresh herbs.
  9. Serve: Serve immediately while warm, or let components cool to room temperature for a refreshing meal. Each diner can mix their bowl to combine all flavors and textures.
  10. Meal Prep Tips: This bowl is perfect for meal prep! Store components separately in airtight containers: quinoa (4 days), roasted veggies/chickpeas (4 days), fresh veggies (2-3 days), dressing (5 days). Assemble when ready to eat.
  11. Nutritional Benefits: This bowl provides complete protein (quinoa + chickpeas), healthy fats (avocado, tahini), complex carbs (sweet potato, quinoa), and 6+ servings of vegetables. The lemon tahini dressing aids nutrient absorption from the greens.
